STATE HONOUR RECIPIENT

"Aralu Mallige" State Award

Awarded by the prestigious Jnana Mandara Academy, Bangalore, for outstanding professional and cultural contribution in classical performing arts.

Overview

The "Aralu Mallige" State Award is one of the most distinguished recognitions bestowed upon artists who have made exceptional contributions to the preservation and promotion of India's classical performing arts. Named after the fragrant jasmine bud — symbolizing the blooming of tradition — this honour celebrates individuals whose artistry has helped keep the cultural heritage of Karnataka vibrantly alive.

Pratham Rai received this award in recognition of his sustained commitment to classical art forms, his dedication to nurturing the next generation of performers, and his efforts in bringing traditional art to modern audiences through innovative performances and community engagement.

Jnana Mandara Academy

The Jnana Mandara Academy, headquartered in Bangalore, is a revered institution dedicated to the advancement of knowledge, arts, and culture across Karnataka. Founded with the vision of identifying and honouring exemplary talent in fields ranging from literature and theater to music and dance, the academy has been instrumental in creating platforms where traditional artists receive the recognition they deserve.

The academy's annual awards ceremony is a celebrated event that brings together scholars, artists, cultural leaders, and dignitaries from across the state. Being recognised by this institution places Pratham among a distinguished lineage of artists who have shaped the cultural narrative of coastal Karnataka and beyond.

What This Award Means for Cultural Preservation

In an era where traditional art forms face existential challenges from rapid modernisation and dwindling audience interest, awards like the "Aralu Mallige" serve a crucial purpose. They shine a spotlight on artists who refuse to let ancient traditions fade, artists who bridge the gap between centuries-old practices and contemporary expression.

For Pratham, receiving this award is not merely a personal achievement but a validation of a larger mission — to ensure that the dance-drama traditions, the elaborate costumes, the soul-stirring music, and the timeless mythological narratives of coastal Karnataka continue to inspire generations to come. It represents the collective effort of gurus, fellow performers, families, and communities who keep these art forms breathing.

The recognition also amplifies visibility for these art forms at a state and national level, encouraging young aspiring artists to take up traditional performance arts with pride and purpose.
